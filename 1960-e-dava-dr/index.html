Multi-Counter Strategy Considerations

  • Asad delivers the highest net ($816,170) and carries the strongest pre-qualification ($1.35M, 59% above purchase price). No seller-paid buyer agent comp and no concessions make this the cleanest offer. However, no seller compensation addendum was included, so the buyer's agent arrangement should be confirmed.
  • Gittings/Jabjiniak nets fourth ($809,020) — just $350 behind the tied pair. They reduced buyer agent comp to 2.5% and moved COE up to April 21, the earliest close by 9+ days. The $15,000 appraisal gap coverage, largest down payment (24.9%), and lowest LTV make this the strongest conventional financing package.
  • Cazier nets fifth ($798,220) despite the additional 1% listing commission for unrepresented buyers. The $8,500 in concessions and $950 home warranty reduce the gap. Pre-qualification at $800K is slightly below their $807.5K loan amount, which warrants attention.
  • Lu/Nguyen ties for second ($809,370) after raising price to $870,000 and eliminating $10K in concessions. However, their pre-qualification ($765K) is now $18K below the loan amount ($783K), which is a financing risk flag. Still the latest close (May 5) and lowest down payment (8.9%).
  • Ayoub also ties for second ($809,370) after raising price to $870,000 with a $20K appraisal gap coverage — the largest shortfall protection of any offer. The 20% down payment and solid conventional financing make this the stronger of the two tied offers from a lending perspective.
  • The top 4 are remarkably tight — Asad ($816,170), Lu/Nguyen and Ayoub (tied $809,370), and Gittings ($809,020) are separated by only $7,150. Cazier trails at $798,220. Key differentiators: Asad has zero seller costs but no comp addendum; Gittings has the fastest close (April 21) and lowest LTV; Ayoub has the largest appraisal gap coverage ($20K) with strong financing; Lu/Nguyen has the highest price but weakest loan profile.
